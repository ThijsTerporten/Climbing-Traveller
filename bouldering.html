The Climbing Traveller

Your guide to your next climbing adventure.

Bouldering

Are you looking to find your next awesome climbing trip? Here are 3 must visit places!

Don't forget that you might need some new gear or a pair of new shoes for your trip! Make sure to use the Gear Up! section to find your new equipment.

Céüse

All around know as one of the best sport-climbing crags in the world it has something for everyone. The crag offers 100's of routes to choose from ranging from 4 all the way to multiple 8c and higher routes. Most recently German Legend Alex Megos completed his project "Bibliographie" which he graded as a 9C! Dare you to try that one! The cliff is located on the top of a hill giving you an awesome view once you top out your route. Most routes are more than 35m long so a rope with a length of 70m+ and around 15 quickdraws is recommended.

Flatanger

Something completely different from the usual warm and sunny locations is this small province in Norway. Get ready to unleash your inner viking climbing one of the many steep overhanging routes. Home to the world's first 9B+ AND 9C don't be intimidated by this since 'Flatanger Cave' has 100+ routes ranging from 4C to the before mentioned 9C grade. And with names for routes like 'Odin's Eye' and 'Thor's Hammer'. Who wouldn't like to give this a shot. Requirements for most routes would be a rope with a length of 70m+ and around 15-20 quickdraws just to be safe.

Margalef

Even though it's especially known for super hard routes and sends, you're guaranteed to find plenty of high quality routes also for beginners and intermediate climbers - there's about 600 routes in 5th and 6th grade! There are over 500 routes of all types and the quality is awesome: big overhangs, big holds, small pockets, tufas, bouldery routes, power endurance, stamina... and many are 5 star quality. Requirements for most routes would be a rope with a length of 70m+ and around 15-20 quickdraws just to be safe.
